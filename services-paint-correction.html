What Paint Correction Actually Does

The swirl marks you see under direct sunlight aren't scratches through your paint — they're micro-marring in the clear coat, caused by improper washing, automatic car washes, and contact with abrasive materials. The good news: they can be removed.

Paint correction uses machine polishers with progressively finer compounds and pads to carefully level the clear coat, eliminating the defects that catch light and scatter it. The result is paint that reflects cleanly — deep, wet, and glassy rather than hazy and dull.

Before we start any correction, we measure the paint thickness on every panel. This ensures we're working safely and never removing more clear coat than necessary. You'll see before/after photos of every vehicle we correct.

Paint Correction FAQ

Paint correction removes defects in the clear coat layer. If a scratch goes through the clear coat into the base coat (you can feel it with your fingernail), it cannot be polished out. We identify these before we start so there are no surprises.

The correction itself is permanent — once a swirl is removed, it's gone. How long your paint stays corrected depends on how you care for it. Automatic car washes reintroduce swirls quickly. Proper hand washing and ceramic coating protection can keep corrected paint looking right for years.
